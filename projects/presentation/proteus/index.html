How prototyping showed complexity in a simple idea.
Project: Guided Forms
Team Size: 4
Manager, Designer, Researcher, Prototyper (Me)
Timeline: 2 months
My Timeline: 2 weeks for version 1, 2 weeks of research.
Project went through 2 major and ~10 minor design changes.
Problem Statement: Make complex forms easier to sign
Target Users: Heavy senders and template users, e.g. HR.

Project: Guided Forms
Research Findings: Only 2 of 16 participants were able to understand the feature.
Any further design iterations were not going to solve the core problem.
Learnings: This project showcases the key role I played in experimental features.
By comprehensive prototyping I saved months of development work that would have potentially led to failure.
I also made sure left no stone was left un-turned and we were able to put to rest this specific design plan.
While the design failed, the work did highlight the system failure: DocuSign doesn’t enforce naming of labels.
Closing
I don't just replicate design, I make sure I fill in the gaps and elevate the design.
I make sure to understand the style and intent of the designer and work as a partner to increse the effciency of the design.
I use look-ahead method to predict how the design might change and build mini-systems to service the project throughout it's life-cycle.
I learn programming in the moment and per project.
By using systems thinking I am able to work on 2-3 projects at the same time.
